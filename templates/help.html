Getting Started

Welcome to TestBank! Here's the typical workflow:

  1. Import questions from your textbook's Word (.docx) test bank files
  2. Review & edit any questions that need fixing in the Question Browser
  3. Add students via CSV import in the Student Roster
  4. Create assignments by auto-generating or hand-picking questions
  5. Publish the assignment to make it visible to students
  6. Monitor progress and grade free-response answers
Importing Questions

Go to Questions in the navigation bar and click "Import .docx".

  1. Upload a .docx test bank file (e.g., "Chapter 4.docx" from Pearson)
  2. The system parses the file and shows a preview: chapter, number of questions, types breakdown, sections found
  3. If the chapter already exists, it will update existing questions — not duplicate them
  4. Click "Confirm Import" to proceed, or "Cancel" to go back
Supported format: Pearson testbank .docx files with the standard format (question number, choices A-E, Answer, Diff, Section, Skill metadata). Images and tables are also extracted.

You can also import via command line: python3 manage.py import_testbank "chapter 4.docx"

Managing Questions

The Question Browser lets you browse, filter, and edit your entire question pool.

Filters
  • Chapter — filter by textbook chapter
  • Section — narrow to a specific section within the chapter
  • Difficulty — Easy (1), Medium (2), Hard (3)
  • Skill — Conceptual, Definition, or Analytical
  • Type — Multiple Choice, Numeric, or Free Response
  • Search — full-text search within question text
Editing Questions

Click the "Edit" button on any question to:

  • Fix question text, change type/difficulty/skill
  • Edit MC choices and set the correct answer
  • Set or update the numeric answer value
  • Edit or remove the context/reference information
  • Update the explanation shown to students after submission

Each question has a unique ID (e.g., CH4-037) for easy reference.

Creating Assignments
Assignment Details
  • Title — e.g., "Homework 1 - Time Value of Money"
  • Mode — "Assignment" (graded) or "Practice" (ungraded)
  • Due Date — optional deadline
  • Randomize — shuffle question order and MC choice order per student
Two Methods
Auto-Generate

Set the number of questions. Use the chapter, section, difficulty, skill, and type filters on the right. The system randomly draws from the matching pool. Each student gets a different random set.

Hand-Pick

Browse all questions with search and filters. Check the ones you want. Sort by ID, Section, Difficulty, or Type. The selected count shows at the bottom. All students get these exact questions (order is randomized if enabled).

Publishing & Assigning

After creating an assignment, it starts as a Draft (not visible to students).

  1. From the Dashboard, click "Publish" next to the assignment
  2. This automatically assigns questions to all students (randomized if enabled)
  3. Students will see it on their Dashboard and Assignment list
  4. You can "Unpublish" to hide it again (students who started can still finish)
Grading
  • Multiple Choice — auto-graded instantly when student selects an answer
  • Numeric — auto-graded with a 1% tolerance (configurable per question)
  • Free Response — requires manual grading. Go to Dashboard → "Grade Free Response" to see the grading queue. You can mark each answer as correct/incorrect and add feedback.

Scores update automatically when you grade free-response answers.

Monitoring Students

Click any assignment title on the Dashboard to see:

  • Per-student status (Not Started / In Progress / Completed)
  • Scores and completion times
  • Click "View Answers" to see a student's detailed responses — each question, their answer, right/wrong, and time spent

The Dashboard also shows:

  • Score Distribution chart for the latest assignment
  • Student Summary table with average scores and total time across all assignments
Student Roster
Adding Students

Go to Students in the nav. Upload a CSV file with columns:

username, first_name, last_name, student_id, email

The system auto-generates passwords and downloads a credential sheet (CSV) for you to distribute to students. Students must change their password on first login.

Managing Students
  • Reset Password — generates a new random password (shown once)
  • Remove — deletes the student account and all their data
Backup & Restore

From the Question Browser page header:

  • Backup — downloads the entire database as a .sqlite3 file. Do this regularly!
  • Restore — upload a previously downloaded backup to replace the current database. Warning: this overwrites everything.
Recommendation: Download a backup before each major import or at least weekly. Store backups in a safe location.

Getting Started
  1. Log in with the username and password provided by your instructor
  2. If this is your first login, you'll be asked to change your password
  3. Your Dashboard shows active assignments and recent results
  4. Click "Start" on any assignment to begin
Taking Assignments
  1. Click "Start" on an assignment from your Dashboard or Assignments page
  2. Questions are shown one at a time
  3. Use the numbered buttons on the left to navigate between questions
  4. The timer tracks how long you spend on each question
  5. The progress bar shows how many questions you've answered
  6. You can go back and change answers before submitting
  7. On the last question, click "Submit Assignment" when you're done
Note: Your answers are saved automatically as you go. If your browser closes or you lose connection, you can come back and continue where you left off.
Question Types
Multiple Choice

Click on the answer you think is correct. Your selection is saved instantly (no need to press Save). A checkmark appears on your selected answer. You can change your answer by clicking a different option.

Numeric

Type a number in the input field. You can include dollar signs ($) and commas — they'll be stripped automatically. Click "Save Answer" when done. Your answer is checked against the correct value with a small tolerance.

Free Response

Type your answer in the text box (up to 5,000 characters). Click "Save Answer". These are graded manually by your instructor, so the score may update later.

Viewing Results

After submitting an assignment, you'll see your results:

  • Your total score at the top
  • Each question marked as Correct, Incorrect, or Pending (for free response)
  • For incorrect MC questions, the correct answer is shown
  • The explanation (if available) helps you understand why
  • Any instructor feedback on free-response questions

You can always revisit results from your Dashboard or Assignments page.

Mistake Collection

Every question you get wrong is automatically added to your Mistake Collection.

  • Go to Mistakes in the navigation bar
  • Filter by chapter using the dropdown
  • Click "Re-Practice All" to generate a quiz from your mistakes
  • When you feel confident about a question, click "Mark Mastered" to hide it
  • The practice count shows how many times you've reviewed each question
Analytics

Go to Analytics to see your performance breakdown:

  • Per-Chapter Accuracy — see which chapters you're strong/weak in
  • Difficulty Breakdown — how you perform on Easy/Medium/Hard questions
  • Skill Breakdown — Conceptual vs Definition vs Analytical
  • Progress Over Time — your scores across assignments
  • Weakest Areas — specific sections where you need the most improvement

Use these insights to focus your study time on the areas that matter most.

Tips for Success
  • Complete assignments well before the due date in case of technical issues
  • Review your mistakes regularly — spaced repetition helps retention
  • Use the Analytics page to identify your weak areas before exams
  • Read the explanations for questions you got wrong
  • Your answers are saved as you go — you won't lose progress if your browser closes
  • If a question has reference information (table, scenario), read it carefully before answering
